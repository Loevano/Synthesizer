Robin

Robin is a multivoice spatial synth built around one shared master voice and a speaker-aware voice bank. It is good at wide pads, distributed chord clouds, shifting pulse patterns, stacked detuned ensembles, and moving timbres that stay related while spreading across the output field.

Master bank linked

Recommended use: build one strong master sound first, choose the voice count and routing behavior, then unlink only the voices you want to turn into accents, rhythmic offsets, or local spatial variations.

Programming tip: start with the oscillator bank, shape the shared filter and envelopes, then listen to the spatial allocation before adding local overrides. Keep most voices linked and use unlinking sparingly.

Live state: every voice can be `Enabled` or `Linked to Master`. Linked voices mirror the master template; unlinked voices open full local controls.
